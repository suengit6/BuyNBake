FAQs #1:

"How do i keep my purchases fresh?"

  1. Flour:
    • Store flour in an airtight container to prevent moisture and pests from affecting its quality.
    • Keep it in a cool, dark place, away from direct sunlight.

  2. Sugar:
    • Store sugar in an airtight container to prevent it from hardening due to moisture.
    • Brown sugar tends to harden easily, so adding a slice of bread or a damp paper towel to the container can help keep it soft.

  3. Butter:
    • Keep butter in the refrigerator, tightly wrapped or sealed to prevent it from absorbing odors.
    • You can also freeze butter for longer storage. Thaw it in the refrigerator before using.

  4. Eggs:
    • Store eggs in the refrigerator in their original carton to prevent absorption of odors.
    • Use eggs before their expiration date for the best results.

  5. Baking Powder and Baking Soda:
    • Keep these leavening agents in a cool, dry place.
    • Check their expiration dates regularly, as they can lose effectiveness over time.

  6. Chocolate:
    • Store chocolate in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
    • Keep it sealed in an airtight container to prevent it from absorbing odors.

  7. Nuts and Seeds:
    • Store nuts and seeds in the refrigerator or freezer to prevent them from going rancid.
    • Bring them to room temperature before using.

  8. Vanilla Extract:
    • Keep vanilla extract in a cool, dark place, away from heat and sunlight.
    • Make sure the bottle is tightly sealed to prevent evaporation.

  9. Milk and Dairy:
    • Refrigerate milk, buttermilk, and other dairy products according to their recommended storage guidelines.
    • Check expiration dates and use them before they expire.

  10. Yeast:
    • Store yeast in the refrigerator or freezer to prolong its shelf life.
    • Ensure that the container is airtight to prevent moisture.
